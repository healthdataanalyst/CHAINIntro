FAQ

Straight answers.
No fine print.

Do I receive a certificate? +
Yes. Every paid program includes a certificate of completion issued upon finishing your capstone project and meeting attendance requirements. But the certificate is not the point — it's a by-product. What you actually walk away with is a GitHub portfolio of production-grade systems, real project documentation, and demonstrated analytical skills that employers can verify. The portfolio is what gets you hired. The certificate is what you frame.
What is the refund policy? +
100% money-back guarantee: If you attend ≥85% of live sessions, complete all assignments on time, and still cannot build a working dashboard — you get a full refund. No questions asked.

Before cohort starts: Full refund up to 7 days before; 50% refund 3–6 days before; no refund within 48 hours.
After cohort starts: 50% refund if you withdraw within the first 7 days; no refunds after Week 1.
If you miss attendance without documented reasons → no refund. Re-entry is at the mentor's sole discretion. One-time deferral available for documented emergencies (valid 6 months).
Do I need Python, SQL, or Power BI before starting? +
No. All Observer participants receive a curated Technical Prep Track with free resources to master SQL and Power BI basics before your first project. If you're completely new, expect to invest extra time mastering fundamentals independently. Biweekly 1-on-1 sessions (paid programs) help clarify doubts throughout.
Do I need healthcare experience? +
No healthcare background required. We teach US healthcare systems (payer vs provider), EHR systems, population health concepts, terminology (HEDIS, FQHC, ICD-10), and regulatory context (HIPAA, CMS) — all integrated into project work.
How much time do I really need each week? +
Observer: Self-paced (your schedule). Associate Analyst: 15–18 hours/week. Flagship Residency: 12–15 hours/week. If you're new to programming or tools, add 3–5 hours/week for foundational learning in parallel.
What is the attendance requirement? +
Observer (free): None whatsoever. The material is yours. Study when you want, skip what you want, come back whenever. Nobody tracks you, nobody chases you. Self-study and win — that's the deal.

Paid mentorship programs: 85% or higher attendance at live sessions and weekly reporting compliance are required. You're in a simulated professional environment — deadlines matter. Missing unexcused sessions may result in removal from the cohort without refund.
Can I use this for college credit or an internship? +
Yes. The Associate Analyst program is designed to serve as a college capstone project or internship. You'll work with real government data from publicly available sources and build portfolio-ready systems. Many students receive academic credit — check with your institution.
What if I get stuck or don't understand something? +
Paid participants have biweekly 1-on-1 sessions with Ryan, async support via Discord, starter code templates, and reference architectures. Observer participants have Discord community access for peer support. Getting stuck is part of learning — the support structure is designed to help you work through it.
Will this guarantee me a job? +
We don't guarantee employment — no ethical training program can. But we provide what employers actually look for: real project experience, portfolio evidence, professional communication skills, domain knowledge, and hands-on system-building capability proven through GitHub repositories.
Are scholarships or regional pricing available? +
Yes — this is a core commitment, not an afterthought. Participants from different countries pay different prices based on purchasing power parity. Someone in India, Nigeria, or Indonesia should not pay the same as someone in the US or UK — that would not be fair access.

Partial scholarships (20–50% reduction) are available for candidates from low-income backgrounds or emerging economies. State your situation honestly during application. We evaluate each case individually and will find a way to make it work if you're genuinely committed.
Which program should I choose? +
Observer: Testing if this approach works for you — no financial commitment. Associate Analyst: Building your first strong portfolio piece for entry-level roles. Flagship Residency: Comprehensive readiness across multiple domains and role rotations for mid-level positions.
